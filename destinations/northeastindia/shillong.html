Shillong - Scotland of the East

Nestled in the East Khasi Hills of Meghalaya, Shillong is a picturesque hill station known for its rolling hills, pleasant climate, and vibrant culture. Often referred to as the "Scotland of the East," this charming city offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, colonial heritage, and modern amenities.

Top Attractions:
  • Umiam Lake: A stunning man-made reservoir offering boating and water sports
  • Elephant Falls: A three-tier waterfall surrounded by lush greenery
  • Shillong Peak: The highest point in Shillong offering panoramic views of the city
  • Ward's Lake: A horseshoe-shaped artificial lake with a wooden bridge and gardens
  • Don Bosco Museum: A seven-story museum showcasing the culture of Northeast India
Local Cuisine:
  • Jadoh: A traditional Khasi rice dish cooked with pork or chicken
  • Dohneiiong: Pork cooked with black sesame seeds
  • Tungrymbai: Fermented soybean dish with a unique flavor
  • Momos: Steamed dumplings filled with meat or vegetables
Best Time to Visit:

The ideal time to visit Shillong is between September and May. The monsoon season (June to August) brings heavy rainfall, which can make sightseeing difficult. Spring (March to May) offers pleasant weather with blooming flowers, while autumn (September to November) provides clear skies and comfortable temperatures.
