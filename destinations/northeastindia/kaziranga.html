Kaziranga National Park – A Wildlife Paradise

Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is located in Assam and is renowned for its incredible biodiversity. The park is home to the world's largest population of the one-horned rhinoceros, as well as tigers, elephants, wild water buffalo, and swamp deer. Spanning across vast floodplains of the Brahmaputra River, it offers lush landscapes, marshlands, and dense forests, making it a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

Top Attractions in Kaziranga:
  • One-Horned Rhinoceros: Spot the rare and majestic Indian rhino in its natural habitat.
  • Elephant Safari: Experience an early morning ride through the grasslands.
  • Jeep Safari: Explore deep into the core areas of the park.
  • Bird Watching: Witness diverse species including migratory birds.
  • Agricultural Fields: Visit local tea gardens and Assamese villages nearby.
Best Time to Visit:

November – April: The park is open for visitors, offering the best wildlife viewing opportunities.

How to Reach Kaziranga?
  • By Air: Nearest airport – Jorhat (Assam), Guwahati.
  • By Train: Nearest railway station – Furkating Junction.
  • By Road: Well-connected via national highways from major cities in Assam.

Kaziranga Itinerary – 3 Days & 2 Nights

Day 1: Arrival & Evening Safari
  • Arrive at Kaziranga and check into a jungle resort.
  • Relax and explore the nearby villages and tea gardens.
  • Enjoy an evening Jeep Safari in the Western Range of the park.
  • Overnight stay in Kaziranga.
Day 2: Wildlife Safari & Cultural Experience
  • Early morning Elephant Safari for close encounters with wildlife.
  • Visit the Kaziranga National Orchid & Biodiversity Park.
  • Explore local tribal villages and enjoy Assamese cuisine.
  • Attend a traditional Bihu dance performance.
  • Overnight stay in Kaziranga.
Day 3: Departure
  • Morning bird-watching session.
  • Check-out and transfer to the airport or railway station.
